Kitchen Cleaner vs Multi-Surface: What's Different?

Kitchen cleaners contain degreasers ‚Äî surfactants that break down oils and fats. They're designed for cooker splashes, greasy worktops, and food residue.

Multi-surface cleaners are gentler and better for dust, fingerprints, and light dirt. They'll struggle with proper cooking grease.

For kitchens, you want a proper degreaser. Save the multi-surface for living rooms and bedrooms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need antibacterial kitchen cleaner?

For surfaces that touch raw meat, yes ‚Äî regular degreasers don't kill bacteria. For general worktops and appliances, degreasing alone is usually enough as long as you're wiping regularly.

Can I use kitchen cleaner on my oven?

Degreasers like Elbow Grease work on oven doors and the exterior. For inside a dirty oven, you'll need a specialist oven cleaner ‚Äî kitchen sprays aren't strong enough for baked-on oven grime.

Is white vinegar a good kitchen cleaner?

It's decent for light cleaning and great for limescale around taps. But it's not as effective as proper degreasers for cooking grease, and it can damage granite, marble, and some grouts over time.

How often should I deep clean the kitchen?

Daily: Wipe worktops and hob after cooking. Weekly: Clean appliance fronts, inside microwave, sink. Monthly: Extractor fan, inside fridge, oven exterior. Quarterly: Deep clean oven interior.
