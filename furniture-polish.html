Polish vs Wax vs Oil: What's the Difference?

Polish (spray): Quick shine and dust protection. Good for regular maintenance on modern furniture. Contains silicones that create a protective layer.

Wax: Traditional finish that soaks into wood. Better protection, richer finish, but more effort. Best for antiques and quality pieces.

Oil: Penetrates and nourishes bare or oiled wood (not varnished). For teak, oak, and unsealed wood furniture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can furniture polish damage wood?

Modern spray polishes contain silicones that can build up over time, making wood look cloudy. This is mainly a concern for antiques. For everyday furniture, the buildup takes years and can be removed with a wood cleaner if needed.

How often should I polish furniture?

Dust weekly with a dry or slightly damp cloth. Polish monthly for regular furniture, or every 2-3 months for wax-finished pieces. Over-polishing causes buildup.

Can I use furniture polish on kitchen cabinets?

Yes, spray polish works fine on most kitchen cabinets. For heavy grease, use a kitchen degreaser first, then polish. Avoid polish on cabinets near the hob ‚Äî it can become sticky with cooking residue.

What about homemade polish?

Olive oil and lemon juice is a popular DIY recipe. It works short-term but can go rancid and attract dust. For occasional use it's fine; for regular care, proper polish is more reliable.
