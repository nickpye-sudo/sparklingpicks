Your Questions, Answered

Do I still need rinse aid with all-in-one tablets?

Depends on your water. In soft water areas, probably not. In hard water areas (London, most of the South East), you'll likely get better results adding rinse aid separately. The "all-in-one" rinse aid helps, but isn't as effective as the liquid stuff.

Can I use half a tablet for small loads?

I've tried this. It doesn't work well ‚Äî the tablets are designed as a single dose and cutting them in half messes with how they dissolve. Just wait until you have a full load, or accept you're using a whole tablet.

Why do my glasses come out cloudy?

Almost always hard water limescale or too much detergent. Try adding rinse aid, check your salt levels, and don't overload the machine. If it's really bad, run an empty hot wash with a dishwasher cleaner.

Are expensive tablets worth it?

Sometimes. Finish and Fairy work noticeably better than the cheapest options. But the Aldi test showed you don't need to spend 25p+ per wash. Our honest advice: try the Aldi tablets first. If they work for you, you'll save ¬£50+ per year.
