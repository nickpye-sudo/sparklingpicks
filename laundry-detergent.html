Bio vs Non-Bio: The Quick Explanation

Bio contains enzymes that break down protein stains (food, blood, grass). Better cleaning, but can irritate sensitive skin.

Non-Bio is gentler ‚Äî recommended for babies, eczema sufferers, and anyone who reacts to bio. Slightly less powerful on tough stains.

Not sure? Start with bio. Switch to non-bio only if you notice irritation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I use liquid, powder, or pods?

Liquid: Best for pre-treating stains and cold washes. Our top recommendation for most people.

Powder: Better value, great on whites, but can leave residue in cold washes.

Pods: Convenient but expensive. Good if you tend to overdose liquid.

Can I wash everything at 30¬∞C?

For most loads with modern detergents, yes. But do a 60¬∞C wash occasionally for towels and underwear ‚Äî it kills bacteria that cold water doesn't.

Why does my washing smell musty even after washing?

Probably your machine, not your detergent. Run an empty hot wash with a machine cleaner monthly. Also: don't leave wet clothes sitting in the drum.

Is bio detergent bad for clothes?

No. The enzymes in bio detergent are designed to break down protein stains, not fabric. Your clothes are safe. The only reason to avoid bio is if it irritates your skin.
