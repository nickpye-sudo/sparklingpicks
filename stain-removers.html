The Golden Rule of Stain Removal

Speed is everything. A fresh stain is 10x easier to remove than a set one. The moment something spills:

  1. Blot (don't rub!) excess liquid
  2. Rinse with cold water from the back of the fabric
  3. Apply stain remover and let it sit
  4. Wash as normal

Never use hot water on protein stains (blood, egg, milk) â€” it cooks them into the fabric permanently.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use bleach on coloured clothes?

Not chlorine bleach â€” it'll strip the colour. Use oxygen bleach (like Vanish Oxi Action pink tub or Ace for Colours) which is colour-safe when used correctly.

Why didn't the stain come out?

Usually because: 1) You used hot water on a protein stain (blood, egg, milk), 2) The stain was already set before treatment, or 3) You put it through the dryer â€” heat sets stains permanently. Always air dry until you're sure it's gone.

Is Vanish better than own-brand oxygen bleach?

Honestly, most oxygen bleaches use similar chemistry (sodium percarbonate). Aldi's and Lidl's versions are nearly as good at half the price. Vanish does have slightly better enzyme blends for specific stains.

Can stain removers damage fabric?

Yes, if misused. Always test on a hidden area first, don't leave on longer than recommended, and never use chlorine bleach on silk or wool. Most oxygen-based removers are safe for regular fabrics.
