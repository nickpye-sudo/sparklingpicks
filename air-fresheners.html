Masking vs Neutralising: What's the Difference?

Masking products add a stronger smell to cover bad odours. When the fragrance fades, the smell returns.

Neutralising products (like Febreze) contain cyclodextrins that trap odour molecules, actually eliminating them.

For persistent odours (cooking, pets, smoke), neutralising products work much better long-term.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are air fresheners bad for you?

Some people are sensitive to synthetic fragrances and may experience headaches or respiratory irritation. If this affects you, try natural options like essential oil diffusers or simply opening windows more often.

What's the most eco-friendly option?

Reed diffusers with natural oils, or simply opening windows. Avoid aerosols where possible. Beeswax candles are another natural option that freshens air while burning.

Why does my air freshener stop working?

You've gone "nose blind" ‚Äî your brain stops registering a constant smell. Try rotating scents, or switch off plug-ins occasionally. Guests will still smell it even when you can't.

How do I remove persistent odours?

First find and eliminate the source. Then use Febreze on affected areas. For carpets and upholstery, sprinkle bicarbonate of soda, leave overnight, then vacuum. For really stubborn smells, professional cleaning may be needed.
